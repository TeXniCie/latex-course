\begin{frame}[fragile]{Tables}
Tables are made in the \textbf{tabular} environment. When making a table you start with \mintinline{latex}{\begin{tabular}}. Afterwards, an extra parameter must be given that tells Latex how the table will be formatted. For example \mintinline{latex}{{l l l}} tells us that we want a table with three columns that are aligned to the left.

Inside of the tabular environment a new column is made with the \&-symbol and a new row with \textbackslash \textbackslash.


\begin{tcolorbox}[width=13cm, title={Example}, size=small]
\begin{tabular}{l | m{8cm}}
\begin{tabular}{l l}
	Name & Population\\
	Amsterdam & 903,399\\
	Rotterdam & 655,468\\
	The Hague & 553,417\\
\end{tabular}
&
\begin{minted}{latex}
\begin{tabular}{l l l}
Name & Population\\
Amsterdam & 903,399\\
Rotterdam & 655,468\\
The Hague & 553,417\\
\end{tabular}
\end{minted}
\end{tabular}
\end{tcolorbox}

\end{frame}
